Annotated Bibliography: Chefs & Restaurant Owners in Kentucky Bourbon and Food Culture
Coomes, Steve. “Pairing Food With Your Bourbon Creates the Ultimate Taste Adventure.” 
Kentucky Bourbon, Kentucky Distillers’ Association, 4 Aug. 2021.
Summary: This article explains how pairing food with bourbon can improve flavor and make the 
dining experience more interesting. Coomes talks about how bourbon has flavors like caramel, 
vanilla, oak, and spice, and how these flavors go well with different foods such as meats, 
desserts, and Southern dishes. The article shows that bourbon is more than just a drink because it 
can also help tell the story of Kentucky’s food culture and traditions. It also explains how pairing 
food and bourbon can create a stronger connection to Kentucky’s identity.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This source is very useful for the stakeholder perspective of chefs and 
restaurant owners because it directly connects bourbon to food and restaurant experiences. In our 
group project, we can use this source to explain how restaurants create meals and pairings that 
represent Kentucky culture. It helps show how chefs use bourbon to make the dining experience 
feel more authentic and connected to the region’s traditions.
Edge, John T. “The New Southern Table.” The New York Times Magazine, 2018.
Summary: This article explains how Southern food has changed over time while still keeping its 
connection to tradition. Edge talks about how modern chefs take classic Southern dishes and 
update them by using local ingredients and new cooking styles. He shows how food can reflect 
the history and culture of a place. The article also explains how restaurants try to balance 
keeping traditional recipes while also making food that appeals to modern customers and 
tourists.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This source is useful for our project because it connects directly to the 
idea of preserving tradition while adapting to change. For the stakeholder perspective of chefs 
and restaurant owners, it shows how restaurants help keep Kentucky food traditions alive while 
still changing with modern food culture. We can use this source in our project to explain how 
restaurants play a big role in regional storytelling through food.
“Kentucky Bourbon as an Agricultural and Economic Engine.” Kentucky Food & Farm, 
15 June 2023.
Summary: This source explains how Kentucky’s bourbon industry depends on local farming and 
agriculture. It talks about how grains like corn, wheat, and rye grown by Kentucky farmers are 
used in bourbon production and help support the state’s economy. The article also shows how 
bourbon affects other parts of the local economy, including restaurants, tourism, and small 
businesses. It helps connect agriculture, food, and bourbon together as important parts of 
Kentucky’s culture and identity.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This source helps support our project by showing how restaurants and 
chefs are connected to the larger local food system. For my stakeholder perspective, it helps 
explain that chefs rely on local ingredients and regional products to create authentic Kentucky 
dishes. In our final project, this source will help show how food culture is connected to farming, 
bourbon production, and local businesses, which supports the larger storytelling theme of 
regional identity.
Minnick, Fred. Bourbon Curious: A Simple Tasting Guide for the Savvy Drinker. Voyageur 
Press, 2019.
Summary: This book talks about the different flavors found in bourbon and how people can 
better understand and appreciate it. Minnick explains common tasting notes like vanilla, caramel, 
spice, and oak, and he also shows how these flavors work well with different foods. He talks 
about how restaurants use bourbon in drinks, sauces, desserts, and food pairings to create a more 
unique dining experience. The book also connects bourbon tasting to Kentucky’s culture and 
tourism, showing how it has become an important part of the state’s identity.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This source will help support the perspective of chefs and restaurant 
owners because it explains how bourbon affects menu planning and food pairings. In our group 
project, we can use this source to show how restaurants use bourbon to create meals that feel 
connected to Kentucky culture. This source helps explain how chefs use food and drink together 
to tell the story of the region and create a memorable experience for visitors.
Risen, Clay. American Whiskey, Bourbon & Rye: A Guide to the Nation’s Favorite Spirit. 
Sterling Publishing, 2017.
Summary: This book gives background information on the history of bourbon and how it became 
strongly connected to Kentucky. Risen explains how bourbon was developed, how it is made, 
and why it is such an important part of the state’s identity. He also talks about how bourbon has 
grown beyond just being a drink and is now part of tourism, restaurants, and Kentucky culture as 
a whole. The book helps show how bourbon has become part of the overall food and storytelling 
experience in the state.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This source will help our group explain the history behind bourbon and 
why it is such an important part of Kentucky’s identity. For my stakeholder perspective, it helps 
connect chefs and restaurant owners to the larger bourbon culture because many restaurants build 
their menus and experiences around bourbon. This source adds historical context that helps 
support the storytelling part of our project.
Smith, Carson. “Culinary Tourism and Bourbon Culture in Kentucky.” Journal of 
Gastronomy and Tourism, vol. 4, no. 2, 2019, pp. 87–101.
Summary: This article explains how bourbon tourism has helped shape Kentucky’s food and 
restaurant culture. Smith talks about how the popularity of the Kentucky Bourbon Trail has 
increased demand for local dining experiences, bourbon tastings, and restaurant pairings. The 
article explains that restaurants and chefs help make the tourism experience stronger by adding 
bourbon into menus and creating dining spaces that feel connected to Kentucky culture.
Rhetorical / Project Use: This is an important source for my stakeholder because it gives 
academic support for how restaurants help shape Kentucky’s identity and tourism industry. In 
our group project, this source will help explain how chefs and restaurant owners play a big role 
in storytelling through food and hospitality. This source also meets the requirement for a peerreviewed journal article.
